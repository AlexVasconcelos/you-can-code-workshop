Roberta Williams

Game designer, writer, and co-founder of Sierra Entertainment

Roberta Williams is one of the most influential game designers of the 1980s and 1990s.

In 1980, she co-founded On-Line Systems (later Sierra Entertainment) with her husband. That year, they released Mystery House, the first ever graphic adventure game.

She went on to design and write King's Quest, one of the most influential adventure games of all time. She would work variously as writer, designer and director on all seven of the game's sequels until her retirement from game development in 1999.
